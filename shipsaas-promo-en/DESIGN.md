# Design System

## Overview

ShipSaaS uses a dark, premium developer-tool visual language built around a charcoal canvas, luminous product art, and very direct conversion messaging. The layout is mostly single-column with wide breathing space, rounded card modules, and one dominant statement per section. Pink gradient accents push the primary call to action, while cyan, sky blue, emerald, and amber add technical energy without breaking the restrained palette. The overall feel is trustworthy, modern, and sales-focused rather than playful.

## Colors

- **Primary Surface**: `#1A1D23` — page-wide dark charcoal background.
- **Panel Surface**: `#2F3436` — feature cards, pricing panels, and glass modules.
- **Quiet Border**: `#444444` — thin card outlines and dividers.
- **Primary Content**: `#E5E5E5` — standard headings and body text on dark surfaces.
- **Pure Highlight**: `#FFFFFF` — feature-card headings and high-contrast UI labels.
- **Primary CTA**: `#FF99CC` — main buttons, eyebrow labels, and gradient headline accents.
- **Secondary Pink**: `#FD97CB` — alternate pink used in badges and glow blends.
- **Cool Accent**: `#87CEEB` — headline gradient blend and cool technical glows.
- **Success Accent**: `#33CC33` — positive comparison states and launch/speed signals.
- **Proof Accent**: `#FE9A00` — star ratings, urgency chips, and offer highlights.

## Typography

- **Primary Sans**: `Poppins` (400, 500, 600, 700, 800, 900). Everything from hero headlines to pricing copy.
- **Mono Utility**: `Roboto Mono` (400, 500, 700). Terminal lines, repo commands, counters, and technical labels.
- **Hero Scale**: 60px bold headlines on the site. Video should exaggerate this into large 88-128px hero statements.
- **Section Scale**: 48px and 60px headings used repeatedly for conversion beats.
- **Card Scale**: 20-32px bold feature titles, 16-20px body, 12-14px uppercase eyebrow labels with wide tracking.

## Elevation

Depth comes from soft glass-like surfaces, rounded 24-40px panels, quiet 1px borders, and localized blur glows behind major sections. Shadows are subtle and diffuse. The site relies more on color haze, glow rings, and contrast between matte background and illuminated assets than on heavy drop shadows. Rounded devices and pricing containers should feel dense and expensive, never flat.

## Components

- **Gradient Hero Phrase**: Large heading with pink-to-sky-blue gradient on the key promise.
- **Tech Orb Product Art**: Hero visual with glowing ring, logos, and neon ecosystem callouts.
- **Rounded CTA Buttons**: High-contrast pink primary button plus dark outlined secondary button.
- **Trust Cluster**: Avatar row, 4.9 rating stars, and “1,000+ developers” proof line.
- **Feature Grid Cards**: Large rounded cards with icon, strong title, and quiet body copy.
- **Tech Marquee Pills**: Floating pill rows showing stack items like Next.js, Stripe, and Better Auth.
- **Terminal Setup Window**: Faux terminal showing `git clone` and local dev startup.
- **Testimonial Cards**: Rounded quote cards with stars and founder avatars.
- **Split Pricing Panel**: Left price block + right capability checklist in one large container.
- **Final CTA Banner**: Wide dark panel with urgency label, social proof, and primary button.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's

- Use exact dark surfaces with pink and sky accents, not generic “tech blue” defaults.
- Keep corners large and soft so every panel feels premium and productized.
- Make technical proof visible: commands, stack logos, ratings, and quantified time savings.
- Use glows locally around hero art, chips, and CTA moments to create energy.
- Keep motion smooth but assertive: slides, zooms, counter builds, and layered reveals.

### Don'ts

- Do not switch to a light canvas or bright white cards.
- Do not use purple-heavy neon cyberpunk styling; site is calmer and more conversion-driven.
- Do not use serif fonts, decorative scripts, or generic system stacks.
- Do not overfill scenes with abstract particles that overpower product proof.
- Do not turn pricing into a soft ending; this brand closes with a direct buy signal.
